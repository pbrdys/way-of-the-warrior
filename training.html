Kung Fu Styles

At our school, we instruct in the most renowned internal martial arts styles originating from the Wudang Mountains. These styles include Taiji Quan, Bagua Zhang, and Xing Yi Quan, each possessing its own distinctive characteristics. To attain mastery in the art of gong fu, individuals must dedicate time to focus on each of these styles individually. Furthermore, each style has its own Qi Gong System, and in addition, we provide instruction in an overarching system known as Hui Chun Qi Gong. This particular Qi Gong serves as the fundamental basis for the internal aspect of gong fu across all styles and further practices. In terms of the external aspect, we also offer instruction in Gong Fu Basics, which encompasses essential elements such as stances, stretching, body flexibility, and power generation.

Taiji

An art of smooth and harmonious movements, flowing and yielding like water. Movement in stillness, stillness in motion. Yin and Yang are in harmony.

Bagua

"Hand of the 8 Trigrams" the art of walking in circles. The essential feature of the Bagua is the agile step work by running in circles and developing spiral-strength within the movements.

Xing Yi

"Fist of Form and Mind", originally derived from spear techniques, Xing Yi is a very straightforward and explosive forward-pointing style and is therefore traditionally learned as a complement to Bagua Zhang.

Qi Gong
